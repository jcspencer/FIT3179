A Country on Fire

by James Spencer (FIT3179 - Semester 2, 2021)

Just how unusual was the 2019/20 bushfire season in New South Wales?

According to Inspector Ben Shepherd (NSW Rural Fire Service), "An average fire season here in New South Wales is typically at around 300,000 hectares".

The 2019/2020 season led to more than 8 million hectares have being in New South Wales alone.

But where did these fires occur, how big were they and how long did they burn for?



Fires Over Time

To begin looking into the 2019/20 fire season in NSW, it's useful to look at the cause of each fire.

The graph below shows the area caused by each type of fire over time. The massive spike in 2019/20 highlights just how serve this season was for NSW.

By dragging along the mini-chart at the top, you can filter down to a specific time period.

The cause of the majority of fires in the 2019/20 season was Lightning, but Suspicious and Undetermined fires made up a large chunk of fires in the 2019/20 season.

Map











However, the best way to describe the extent of the 2019/20 bushfire season is to visualize them geospatially.

The map on the right shows fires in NSW over time, beginning in 2020.

The fires are grouped by their cause - double click on a cause in the list to see how much damange they caused.

As we saw in the area chart above, this graph solidifies that lightning caused a significant chunk of large fires in the 2019/20 fire season.

Duration of Fires

But how long are each of these fires lasting?

The chart below shows the distribution of the average fire duration for each month post-2000.

Hint: double-click on a category of fire to see how the distribution changes!

44% of fires are extinguished within 72 hours of starting, with 70% being out within 7 days.

However, some single fires lasted over three months!

Deep Dive

There are a number dive of different characteristics of paricular fires that make up a fire season.

This chart shows a bit of information about each major fire in NSW over time. Some characteristics include:

  • The size of the circle shows the size of a particular fire
  • The colour of the circle shows the category of a particular fire

You can also select a time range at the top to zoom into a particular time range.

Finally, the chart below allows you to compare fires in NSW over time.

  • The size of the circle shows the size of a particular fire
  • The colour of the circle shows the category of a particular fire
  • The height of the circle shows the duration of a particular fire

You can also drag along an area of the chart to investigate a particular time period.

Conclusion

In summary, a number of key factors set the 2019/20 NSW bushfire season apart from all of those that had come before it.
